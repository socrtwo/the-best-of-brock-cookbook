How it scales

  • Salt and spices scale sub-linearly (×ratio0.7–0.75) — strong flavors compound when you double a pot.
  • Leavening (baking powder, soda, yeast) scales at ratio0.85 — too much makes baked goods collapse.
  • Eggs round to whole numbers; below 0.4 they are dropped.
  • Aromatics (onion, garlic, celery, carrot) scale sub-linearly to avoid a wall of allium.
  • Liquids, flour, sugar, dairy, and meat scale linearly.
  • Amounts snap to cooking-friendly fractions (½, ⅓, ¼, ⅛) rather than decimals.
